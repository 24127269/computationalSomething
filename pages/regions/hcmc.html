The Fusion of France and Vietnamese Soul

The Perfect Fusion

When the French brought baguette to Vietnam, the Vietnamese transformed it in their own way. By adding rice flour to wheat flour, the crust became crispy and the interior lighter and airier. After 1954, stuffed sandwiches began appearing with pate (French influence), pickled vegetables, herbs, chili, and Vietnamese meats. The result was a dish so perfect it was added to the Oxford Dictionary in 2011.
